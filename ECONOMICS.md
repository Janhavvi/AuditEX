# ECONOMICS

AuditEX should not be viewed as a normal SaaS product with direct subscription revenue. The better way to think about it is as a qualification layer for Credex. The tool identifies startups that already have growing AI-related operational spend and turns that problem into a financial conversation.

The target customer is usually an AI-heavy startup with around 10–80 employees. These companies often adopt tools quickly without putting ownership around procurement or cost controls. Over time, subscriptions stack up across engineering, design, operations, and product teams. By the time somebody notices, the company is already spending thousands every month across overlapping AI products.

The economics work if AuditEX can consistently identify companies with meaningful inefficiencies and move a small percentage of them into Credex’s broader ecosystem.

A reasonable assumption is that a converted customer eventually generates somewhere between $10,000 and $40,000 in total business value depending on the type of financial relationship created. That could include financing, vendor optimization, procurement support, or future credit usage.

Using conservative assumptions:

| Scenario | Revenue | Margin | Gross Profit |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Conservative | $10,000 | 25% | $2,500 |
| Mid-range | $20,000 | 30% | $6,000 |
| Strong account | $40,000 | 35% | $14,000 |

For early-stage modeling, treating a converted customer as worth roughly $2,500–$7,500 in gross profit is realistic.

The acquisition strategy relies heavily on targeted outbound efforts and community-driven distribution instead of paid ads.

Founder and operator outreach is likely the strongest early channel because messaging can be personalized around actual AI tooling problems. Sending 100 highly targeted messages might take around 10 hours. If founder time is valued at $50/hour, the effective acquisition cost is roughly $500 for the campaign.

If those 100 messages generate 15–20 completed audits, the effective CAC becomes:

```txt
$500 / 20 audits = $25 per completed audit
```

Community teardown posts are another efficient channel. Posting anonymized breakdowns showing how startups overspend on AI tools creates curiosity and social proof. A single detailed teardown might take 2–3 hours to prepare and generate 5–15 audits depending on reach and relevance.

That usually results in a CAC between $10–$20 per completed audit.

Partnerships with fractional CFOs and startup finance consultants could become even more efficient over time. These operators already work directly with companies managing burn and software spend. Once trust is established, a single partner could consistently introduce high-quality leads with almost no direct acquisition spend.

The funnel becomes profitable even with modest conversion rates.

A realistic early funnel might look like this:

| Funnel Stage | Conversion |
|---|---:|
| Audit completed → high-savings lead | 20% |
| High-savings lead → consultation booked | 25% |
| Consultation booked → purchase/conversion | 20% |

That means:

```txt
100 completed audits
→ 20 qualified leads
→ 5 consultations
→ 1 converted customer
```

So the overall audit-to-purchase conversion rate is approximately 1%.

At a $2,500 gross profit per converted customer:

```txt
1 customer = $2,500
100 audits = $2,500 value
Each audit ≈ $25 expected value
```

At higher-value customer relationships, the economics improve significantly.

The long-term opportunity becomes meaningful if AuditEX can evolve into a repeatable acquisition engine for AI-heavy startups.

To reach $1M ARR within 18 months:

| ARR per Customer | Customers Needed |
|---|---:|
| $10k ARR | 100 customers |
| $25k ARR | 40 customers |

At a 1% audit-to-purchase conversion rate:

| Customers Needed | Required Audits |
|---|---:|
| 40 | 4,000 audits |
| 100 | 10,000 audits |

If the conversion rate improves to 3%, the required audit volume drops significantly:

| Customers Needed | Required Audits |
|---|---:|
| 40 | ~1,350 audits |
| 100 | ~3,350 audits |

The most important variable is not traffic volume. The biggest lever is whether the audits consistently reveal meaningful savings opportunities.

If companies regularly discover:
- duplicated AI subscriptions
- unnecessary API usage
- overlapping tooling
- unmanaged AI budgets

then AuditEX naturally creates urgency and makes conversion into financial products much easier.

The strongest version of the product is not simply “AI spend analytics.”

It becomes:

> a financial intelligence layer for startups scaling AI usage faster than their operational systems.

That positioning creates stronger retention, higher-value conversations, and better economics than a traditional SaaS dashboard alone.
